# The One Constraint That Could Change Everything

You don't have 47 problems.

It feels like you do. The career, the relationship, the money, the health, the purpose — they all feel stuck at the same time. Like everything is tangled together and you can't pull on one thread without unraveling the rest.

But that's not how it works.

## Constraints, not problems

There's a concept in engineering called the "theory of constraints." The idea is simple: every system has exactly one bottleneck. You can have a hundred things that look broken, but only one of them is actually holding everything else back.

Fix that one thing, and the other 46 problems start moving on their own.

The reason you feel stuck in multiple areas isn't that you have multiple problems. It's that one constraint is creating pressure across your entire life.

## Finding it

The constraint isn't always the thing that hurts the most. It's not always the thing you think about at 3am. Sometimes it's quieter than that.

The constraint is the one thing that, if it changed, would make the biggest number of other things possible.

:::decision-block{question="What's the one thing that, if it changed, would make everything else easier?" blockId="free-l2-q1"}

Now look at what you just wrote. Compare it to what you said in the first lesson about where you feel stuck.

Are they the same thing? Or did you just discover something underneath?

Most people find that the constraint is one level deeper than the surface problem. "I'm stuck in my career" often has a constraint like "I haven't had the conversation with my partner about money." "I can't get healthy" often has a constraint like "I keep saying yes to things that drain me."

:::decision-block{question="Is this constraint something you've been avoiding? What specifically have you been avoiding about it?" blockId="free-l2-q2"}

You now have two things most people never get: a named constraint and an honest look at why you've been avoiding it.
