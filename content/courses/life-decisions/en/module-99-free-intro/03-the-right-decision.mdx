# The Right Decision

You've done something in the last two lessons that most people never do.

You named the area where you're stuck. You identified the one constraint underneath it. You looked honestly at what you've been avoiding.

Now comes the part that actually changes things.

## Why decisions work (and understanding doesn't)

Understanding is comfortable. You can understand a problem for years and nothing changes. Understanding doesn't require risk. It doesn't require telling anyone. It doesn't require doing anything differently tomorrow morning.

A decision is different. A decision creates a before and after. Once you've decided, you can't un-know that you decided. Even if you don't act on it immediately, the decision sits there. It changes how you see everything else.

That's why the self-help industry avoids decisions. Decisions end the loop. Understanding keeps it going.

## The structure of a good decision

A good decision has three parts:

1. **The constraint** — the one thing holding everything else back
2. **The decision** — the specific thing you will do about it
3. **The first action** — what you will do in the next 24 hours

Not a plan. Not a goal. Not a vision board. A decision with a first action.

:::decision-block{question="Given your constraint, what's the one decision that would change everything?" blockId="free-l3-q1"}

You just made a real decision. Not a wish. Not a plan. A decision.

Now — what's the first thing you'll do about it? Not next week. Not when you're ready. What will you do in the next 24 hours that makes this decision real?
