# The Trap You Don't Know You're In

## Luana spent $4,700 on self-help and still hasn't started

Luana wanted to open a hair salon.

She'd been talking about it for two years. She took a business course. Then a marketing course. Then a "find your purpose" workshop. Then she started therapy to understand why she was self-sabotaging.

One day she called me and said:

"I think I finally understand why I haven't started. My therapist says I have a fear of success linked to my relationship with my father."

I asked her a different kind of question.

"Do you know how much it costs to rent a space in your neighborhood?"

Silence.

"Do you know how much salon chairs cost? Do you know what licenses you need?"

More silence.

Two years understanding why she couldn't start. Not a single afternoon figuring out what starting actually looks like.

Luana is one of the smartest people I know. She's not lazy. She's not broken.

**She's trapped. And the trap is invisible.**

## Understanding is not deciding

Here's what happened to Luana. And probably to you.

She felt stuck. She looked for help. Every kind of help said the same thing:

*Understand yourself first.*

So she did.

She understood her fear. She understood her patterns. She understood her childhood. She had language for all of it.

But nothing changed.

Why?

Because understanding is not the same as deciding. They feel similar. They both involve thinking. They both feel like progress.

But understanding keeps you in your head.

Deciding puts you in motion.

Luana understood her fear of success perfectly. She still hadn't Googled the price of a salon chair.

> The understanding was the trap.

## The industry profits from keeping you stuck

Think about every self-help thing you've ever tried.

- **Therapy** gave you language for your pain. Great. But did it tell you what to DO on Monday morning?
- **Energy work** made you feel aligned. For about a week. Then what?
- **Manifestation** gave you a vision board. You stare at it while nothing changes.
- **Courses** gave you frameworks and notes. You felt inspired for 48 hours. Then you moved on.

Every single one of these points backward or upward.

They help you understand the past. Or visualize the future.

**None of them point forward. None of them ask: "So what are you going to do about it? By when?"**

And here's the uncomfortable part.

That's not an accident.

A customer who understands but doesn't act is a customer who comes back next month for more understanding. The business model only works if you stay stuck enough to keep buying.

I know that sounds cynical. So try this:

Add up how much you've spent on self-development in the last three years.

Now count how many actual decisions it produced.

## "I'm working on it in therapy"

You know what the modern version of avoidance sounds like?

"Oh yes, I know I should leave. But I'm working on it in therapy."

How long have you been working on it?

And if you say this out loud to a friend — if you say "maybe you should just decide and do it" — they look at you like you're the crazy one.

The black sheep. The insensitive one.

They tell you that you just haven't found the right practice yet. That you'll find the one that works. That healing takes time.

Here's what I believe, and I'm not going to soften it:

**You just need to make the right decision and commit to it. There is nothing else to be done.**

## Luana finally opened the salon

Not because of therapy. Not because of the courses.

Because one Sunday she got so frustrated that she drove to three neighborhoods, wrote down rent prices, and called a friend who knew a supplier.

That Sunday afternoon produced more progress than two years of understanding.

What changed?

She stopped trying to figure out WHY she was stuck. She started figuring out WHAT comes next.

She didn't need more insight. She needed a phone call, a spreadsheet, and a Saturday afternoon.

That's what a decision looks like. It's not a breakthrough with music playing. It's a phone call on a Sunday.

## Eight patterns that keep you in the same place

I've been watching these in myself, in Indy, in our friends for years. The same patterns show up over and over.

See which ones you recognize:

1. **Solving the safe problem.** You reorganize your closet while the career decision collects dust. You know which problem is the real one. You pick the other one.

2. **Asking someone else what's wrong with you.** Your therapist sees one hour of your week. Only you see the full picture.

3. **Research as a delay tactic.** One more book. One more episode. Not to learn. To avoid deciding for one more day.

4. **Big declarations, zero follow-through.** "I'm going to change everything!" OK. What are you doing tomorrow at 9am?

5. **Perfect plans, zero commitment.** Beautiful spreadsheet. No deadline. No "I will do this by Friday." Theater.

6. **Borrowing someone else's answer.** Her life looks perfect on Instagram. You don't know what she sacrificed. And her situation is not yours.

7. **Only choosing safe things.** If you can easily undo it, it's probably not the one that changes your life.

8. **Doing nothing and calling it patience.** Not deciding IS a decision. The most expensive one.

How many? Three? Five?

Write them down. They'll come back throughout the course.

## What happens next

Knowing about these patterns is the first step. But knowing isn't enough. Luana knew about self-sabotage for two years.

What matters is what's next.

And what's next has to do with something your body has been trying to tell you. Something nobody in self-development talks about.
