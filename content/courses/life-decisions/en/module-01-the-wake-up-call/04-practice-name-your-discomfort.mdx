# Practice: Name Your Discomfort

## This is where the course stops being something you watch

Every module from here on ends with a practice class like this one.

The structure is always the same:

1. Download the docx
2. Fill the questionnaire by hand
3. Use the AI prompt on the last page to sharpen your answers
4. Save the result in your AI project folder

By Module 9, that folder will contain the full story of your decision. The map, the target, the constraint, the plan, the execution log. Everything.

But it starts here. With naming the thing.

## What you're building

This module's document is your **Discomfort Map.**

It answers three questions:
- What's wrong?
- How long has it been wrong?
- What have you tried that didn't fix it?

This isn't a journal entry. It's a diagnostic. Facts, not feelings. Numbers, not narratives.

## How to fill it

Open the docx. Twelve questions. Write full sentences, not bullet points. Be honest. Nobody reads this but you and the AI.

If you rush this, you'll get a clean document and a dirty truth. Take the time.

### Part 1: The signal

These four questions are about what your body is telling you.

**Q1. What physical symptoms showed up in the last two years that weren't there before?**

Insomnia. Weight gain. Fatigue. Headaches. Anxiety attacks. Digestive issues. Chronic tension in your neck or shoulders.

List everything. Even the ones you've been blaming on age or stress.

**Q2. When you're alone and not distracted — no phone, no Netflix, no work — what do you feel?**

Not "fine." Nobody who bought this course feels fine in the silence.

Anxious? Numb? Frustrated? Restless? Sad? Something you can't name?

**Q3. When did this start? Can you trace it to a specific moment?**

Not "a while ago." When? What was happening in your life around that time?

**Q4. What makes it temporarily better?**

Scrolling? Wine? Shopping? Overworking? Working out?

These aren't solutions. They're painkillers. Name them without judgment.

### Part 2: What you've tried

**Q5. List every self-help thing you've tried in the last three years.**

Therapy. Courses. Books. Coaching. Meditation apps. Journaling. Workshops. Retreats. All of it.

**Q6. For each one: what actually changed in your daily life?**

Not what you learned. Not how you felt during. What changed in your behavior, your decisions, your circumstances.

If the answer is "nothing concrete"... write that.

**Q7. How much money have you spent on all of this?**

Rough number. $500? $2,000? $5,000? More?

**Q8. If all of those worked, why are you here?**

This is the hardest question in the docx. Write the real answer.

### Part 3: The thing

**Q9. What is the thing you keep thinking about but not doing?**

The decision you've been circling. The conversation you haven't had. The move you haven't made.

**Q10. How long have you been thinking about it?**

Months? Years? Be specific.

**Q11. What do you tell yourself about why you haven't done it yet?**

Write the exact words. "I'm not ready." "The timing isn't right." "I need more information."

These are the lies. Write them down so you can see them on paper instead of in your head.

**Q12. What would realistically happen if you did it tomorrow?**

Not the catastrophe version. Not the fairy tale version. The realistic version. What would actually happen?

## After answering: the AI step

You've done the hard work. Now let the AI sharpen it.

Copy the prompt below. Paste it into ChatGPT or Claude. Then paste your 12 answers after it.

---

### Copy this prompt:

```
You are a discomfort mapping coach for The Right Decision methodology. I just answered 12 questions about what's wrong in my life, what I've tried, and what I've been avoiding.

Your job is NOT to comfort me. Your job is to make my answers more honest and specific.

Do three things:

1. PUSH FOR HONESTY. Read each of my answers. Wherever I was vague, ask me a follow-up. Examples:
   - If I said "I feel stuck" → ask "Stuck in what specific area? Career? Relationship? Money? Name one."
   - If I said "a while" → ask "How many months? How many years? Be specific."
   - If I said "I'm not ready" → ask "What specifically would make you ready? And has that ever actually arrived?"

2. NAME MY PATTERN. Based on my answers, tell me which of these eight patterns I'm committing (pick the top 2-3):
   - Solving the safe problem instead of the scary one
   - Asking someone else to diagnose what's wrong with me
   - Using research/courses/books as a delay tactic
   - Making big declarations with zero follow-through
   - Building perfect plans without actually committing
   - Copying someone else's life plan
   - Only choosing reversible, safe decisions
   - Not deciding and pretending that's neutral

   Be direct. Quote my own words back to me as evidence.

3. PRODUCE MY DISCOMFORT MAP. Organize my answers into a clean document with these sections:
   - THE SIGNAL: What my body is telling me (physical + emotional symptoms)
   - THE COST: How long this has been going on and what I've spent trying to fix it
   - THE THING: The specific decision I've been avoiding
   - MY PATTERN: Which of the eight patterns are keeping me stuck
   - ONE SENTENCE SUMMARY: "I am stuck because ___."

Be honest with me. I can handle it.

Here are my answers:
```

---

Paste your 12 answers below the prompt. The AI will ask follow-up questions if you were vague. Answer them.

When it produces your Discomfort Map, save it in your "The Right Decision" project folder.
