# Practice: Your Execution Protocol

## The seventh document. How you'll actually live the next 30 days.

You have the plan. You have the decision. Now you need the daily rhythm that turns the plan into action.

This isn't about motivation. Motivation fades. This is about building a machine that runs whether you feel like it or not.

## The questions

Eight questions. Short but important.

### Part 1: The daily rhythm (Questions 1-3)

**Q1. What time will you do your daily task?**

Not "in the morning." A time. "7:30am after coffee." "6:15pm after I close the laptop."

**Q2. Where will you do it?**

Kitchen table. Desk. Coffee shop. The specific chair. The specific spot.

Environment matters more than willpower. If you always do the thing in the same place at the same time, your brain starts doing it automatically.

**Q3. How will you record whether you did it?**

A simple tracker. A note on your phone. A checkmark on a calendar on the wall. A text to your witness that says "done" or "missed."

The method doesn't matter. Doing it every day does. Yes or no. That's all you track.

### Part 2: The minimum viable version (Questions 4-5)

**Q4. What's your full daily version?**

The thing you do on a normal day. "30 minutes of job search after coffee."

**Q5. What's your minimum version for the worst days?**

The smallest possible version. So small it feels almost silly. But it keeps the loop alive.

A bad answer: "30 minutes" (that's the full version, not the minimum)

A good answer: "Open the tab and look at one listing. 2 minutes."

### Part 3: The emotional response plan (Questions 6-8)

**Q6. Which emotion is most likely to hit you during execution?**

Look at the four from last class: fear, grief, shame, exhaustion. Which one is YOUR most likely visitor?

**Q7. What will you THINK when it arrives?**

Write your own script. Not a mantra. A reminder.

A bad answer: "I am strong and capable!" (Affirmation. Your brain doesn't believe it when you're scared.)

A good answer: "This fear means the decision is real. I can feel scared AND do the 30 minutes. Both can happen today."

**Q8. Write your "when X, then Y" response:**

"When [the emotion] shows up, I will [specific response]."

Examples:
- "When fear shows up, I will sit with it for 5 minutes, then open the laptop and do the minimum version."
- "When shame shows up, I will ask: am I closer to my Tuesday than yesterday? If yes, I'm on track."
- "When exhaustion shows up, I will do the minimum version and go to bed without guilt."

This is an if-then plan. You're pre-deciding what to do so that when the emotion hits, you don't have to figure it out in the moment.
