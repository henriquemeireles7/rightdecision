# How You Know It's Done

## Maria's constraint resolved at 7:43pm on a Wednesday

She opened her email after dinner. Subject line: "Offer Letter — Marketing Director."

$6,800/month. A company she'd researched. A product she believed in. A role she chose.

She sat there for a while. Then she texted her witness, Julia: "Got the offer."

Julia replied: "TOLD YOU."

Then Maria did something strange. She felt sad.

Not because the offer was wrong. Because something was ending. The 26-month chapter of "I should leave." The uncomfortable familiar. The job she hated but knew every corner of.

The constraint was resolved. The thing she'd been avoiding was behind her.

And it felt... quiet. Not fireworks. Not a breakthrough moment. Quiet.

## Resolution is not what it looks like in movies

Nobody tells you this part.

The moment you've been building toward — the decision made, the constraint resolved, the Tuesday getting closer — doesn't feel like a celebration.

It feels like relief.

Indy describes it perfectly: "It's a feeling that you are closer to what you want. Like I am already better than yesterday."

Not ecstasy. Not "I made it." Just: I'm closer. I did the thing.

**The reward was never the result. The reward was every morning at the kitchen table, doing the 30 minutes after coffee.** The offer letter was just the confirmation that the system worked.

## Three signs the constraint is resolved

How do you know you're done, not just tired of working on it?

### 1. Your State Map shows measurable change

Not "I feel better." Numbers.

Maria's before and after:

| Domain | Before | After |
|---|---|---|
| Career | 3/10 | 8/10 |
| Finances | 3/10 | 6/10 |
| Health | 4/10 | 7/10 |
| Relationship | 5/10 | 7/10 |
| Emotional | 3/10 | 7/10 |
| **Total** | **18/50** | **35/50** |

Seventeen points. From one decision.

### 2. The daily tasks feel like maintenance, not effort

Early on, the 30-minute coffee habit required discipline. By Week 6, Maria did it on autopilot. She didn't have to decide to do it. She just did it.

When the tasks feel like brushing your teeth — automatic, no willpower required — the habit has installed. The execution is sustaining itself.

### 3. A new discomfort has surfaced

This is the surprising one.

When Maria resolved the career constraint, something she couldn't see before became visible: the relationship needed real attention. Not crisis-level. But the distance with Carlos was now the loudest signal.

The career constraint had been blocking her view. With it resolved, the next constraint appeared.

**This is not failure. This is exactly how the loop works.** One resolved constraint reveals the next one.

## The trap of premature resolution

"I feel better, so I must be done."

Be careful with this.

Feeling better is not the same as resolving the constraint. You might feel better because:

- The novelty of taking action created temporary energy
- You told your witness and felt accountable
- The first few tasks gave you a dopamine hit

All real. All temporary.

**Resolution means the State Map changed, the tasks feel automatic, and a new constraint surfaced.** All three. Not one. Not two. All three.

If the tasks still require effort and your State Map hasn't moved — you're in execution, not resolution. Keep going.

*Look at your data. Are you resolved, or still executing?*
