# Holding the Line

## Resolved constraints come back if you let them

A friend of mine lost 30 pounds. Changed his diet. Started running. Felt amazing.

Six months later he'd gained 25 back.

Not because he didn't know what to do. He knew exactly what to do. He'd done it.

But the habits decayed. The running stopped when he got busy. The diet loosened during the holidays. One skipped week became a skipped month.

The constraint was resolved. Then it unresolved.

## Why constraints regress

Three reasons:

### 1. The habit lost its trigger

The running was "after work at 6pm." Then he changed jobs and the new commute shifted his schedule. The trigger broke. The habit broke with it.

**Fix:** When your life changes, relocate the trigger. Don't wait for the habit to die. The moment your schedule shifts, ask: "Where does this habit live now?"

### 2. The urgency disappeared

When the constraint was active, the motivation was high. The discomfort was loud. Once it resolved, the discomfort stopped. And without discomfort, the daily effort felt optional.

"I already fixed this. I don't need to keep doing the thing."

Yes, you do. The resolved state requires maintenance. Not the same intensity as the execution phase. But the habit that got you here needs to continue, or the constraint comes back.

**Fix:** The habit from Module 6 doesn't stop when the constraint resolves. It downsizes. From 30 minutes to 15. From daily to 3x/week. But it never goes to zero.

### 3. Success-induced complacency

"Things are good now. I can relax."

Yes. You earned the relaxation. But relaxation and abandonment are different things.

Maria got the new job. $6,800/month. Energy back. Sleeping well.

What she maintained: the Sunday review habit (reduced from 15 minutes to 5). What she kept: an updated resume on her computer. What she continued: coffee conversations with people in her industry.

She didn't need to job-search anymore. But she kept the infrastructure that made the decision possible.

## The maintenance checklist

After resolving a constraint, answer three questions:

**1. Which habit from this cycle do I keep? In what reduced form?**

The habit that sustained execution doesn't stop. It downsizes.

"30 minutes of job search after coffee" → "15 minutes of industry reading after coffee, 3x/week"

**2. What are the regression signals?**

Name the specific early warnings that the constraint is returning.

Maria's: "If I start dreading Monday again. If my sleep drops below 6 hours. If I haven't talked to anyone outside my company in 3 weeks."

**3. What do I do when I see a signal?**

Not "restart the whole course." Just restart the review habit. Pull out the State Map. Check the numbers.

Catching a regression in Week 1 takes an hour to fix. Catching it in Month 6 takes another full cycle.

*What are YOUR regression signals for the constraint you're resolving?*
