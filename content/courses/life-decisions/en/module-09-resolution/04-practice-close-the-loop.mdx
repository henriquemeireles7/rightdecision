# Practice: Close the Loop, Open the Next

## The ninth and final document. The end of this cycle. The beginning of the next.

This practice does two things:

1. Closes the current cycle with evidence
2. Opens the next one with a new throughline decision

It's the last document of THIS loop. And the first document of the NEXT one.

## Part 1: Close the loop

### Resolution check (Questions 1-5)

**Q1. Pull up your original State Map from Module 2. Now write your current numbers for each domain.**

| Domain | Module 2 (Before) | Now (After) | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Money | /10 | /10 | +/- |
| Career | /10 | /10 | +/- |
| Relationships | /10 | /10 | +/- |
| Health | /10 | /10 | +/- |
| How you feel | /10 | /10 | +/- |
| **Total** | /50 | /50 | +/- |

Fill it in with real numbers. This is your proof.

**Q2. Look at your Decision Statement from Module 5. Did you do it?**

Not "I worked on it." Did the specific thing happen by the specific date?

A clear yes or no.

If yes — you made the right decision.

If partially — what happened? What remains?

**Q3. Look at your Target State from Module 3. How much closer is your actual Tuesday to your ideal Tuesday?**

Compare the specific conditions. Which ones are now real? Which ones are still aspirational?

**Q4. What surprised you about the process?**

Something you didn't expect. Something the methodology revealed that you couldn't have predicted at Module 1.

Maria: "I thought the hard part would be the interviews. It wasn't. The hard part was Day 3 in the parking lot. Once I got past that, everything else felt inevitable."

**Q5. What would you do differently if you ran this cycle again?**

Not "I'd be more disciplined." Specific changes.

Maria: "I would narrow my target companies earlier. I wasted two weeks applying to places I didn't care about."

### Maintenance setup (Questions 6-7)

**Q6. Which habit from this cycle do you keep? In what form?**

Write the reduced version.

"30 minutes of job search after coffee" → "15 minutes of industry reading after coffee, 3x/week."

**Q7. What are your regression signals? What will you do when you see one?**

List 2-3 early warnings. And the response.

- "If I start dreading Monday → pull up the State Map and check the career numbers"
- "If sleep drops below 6 hours → something is off, review the five domains"

## Part 2: Open the next loop

### New cycle setup (Questions 8-10)

**Q8. Now that this constraint is resolved, what's the next discomfort?**

It's probably already visible. The thing that was hiding behind the old constraint.

Maria: "The relationship. Now that I'm not exhausted and resentful from work, I can see clearly that Carlos and I need to have a real conversation about what we both want."

**Q9. Write your new throughline decision.**

Same format as the Onboarding class: "The decision I'm working on next is ___."

Not a goal. Not a wish. A decision you need to make.

**Q10. Which module do you re-enter the loop at?**

You don't restart at Module 1 unless everything has changed. Use the entry-point logic:

- I know something is wrong but can't name it → **Module 1**
- I know what's wrong but don't know where I want to be → **Module 3**
- I know the gap but haven't identified the constraint → **Module 4**
- I know the constraint but haven't committed → **Module 5**
- I've committed but don't have a plan → **Module 6**

Most people on Cycle 2 start at Module 3 or Module 4. You already know how to map your state. You just need to find the new constraint.

## After answering: the AI step

The final AI prompt. It validates your resolution and sets up the next cycle.

```

3. CHECK THE MAINTENANCE PLAN. Is my reduced habit specific enough? Are my regression signals concrete enough to actually notice? If vague, help me sharpen them.

4. SET UP THE NEXT CYCLE. Look at my new throughline decision. Is it a real decision (verb + date potential) or a goal disguised as a decision? Recommend which module to re-enter at.
```
