# Practice: Define Your Target State

## Your third document. The one that answers: "Where am I going?"

You now have two documents in your project folder:

1. **Discomfort Map** (Module 1) — what's wrong
2. **State Map** (Module 2) — where you actually are

This class adds the third: **your Target State.** Where you want to be. Not as a fantasy. As a set of conditions you can observe, test, and measure.

After this, you'll have the full picture: where you are and where you want to be. The gap between them is what Module 4 will help you navigate.

## How to fill the questionnaire

Download the Module 3 docx. Fifteen questions. Write in full sentences.

### Part 1: The Tuesday Test (Questions 1-6)

**Q1. Describe your ideal Tuesday morning.**

What time do you wake up? Naturally or by alarm? What's the first thing you do? Who's there?

**Q2. Describe your ideal Tuesday midday.**

What are you working on? Did you choose it? Where are you?

**Q3. Describe your ideal Tuesday evening.**

What do you do when work ends? Who are you having dinner with? What do you feel before sleep?

**Q4. What's the one thing ABSENT from your ideal Tuesday that's present in your current one?**

The obligation. The dread. The person. The commute. Name it.

**Q5. What's PRESENT in your ideal Tuesday that's absent now?**

The freedom. The energy. The person. The activity.

**Q6. Read your Tuesday out loud. What do you feel?**

Longing? Fear? Impatience? Excitement? Nothing?

If nothing — it's not yours. Try again.

### Part 2: Conditions with evidence (Questions 7-11)

**Q7. Write 3-5 conditions that define your target state.**

Format each one like this:

> "I [observable situation]. Evidence: [how I know it's true]."

Example:
> "I work on projects I chose. Evidence: I can decline any meeting without financial consequence."

Example:
> "I don't worry about money for basics. Evidence: I buy groceries without checking my bank app."

**Q8. For each condition, what number would indicate you're getting closer?**

Not the target itself. The checkpoint. The metric that serves the condition.

**Q9. Which condition matters MOST to you?**

If you could only achieve one, which one?

**Q10. Which condition are you most afraid of pursuing?**

That's probably the real one.

**Q11. Is there a condition you wrote that you wouldn't want if nobody was watching?**

If yes, cross it out. It's performance, not desire.

### Part 3: The ownership check (Questions 12-15)

**Q12. For each condition: where did this desire first come from?**

Your own experience? A parent? A friend? A post you saw? Trace it.

**Q13. Would you still want this on a desert island?**

Removes status-based desires. What remains is real.

**Q14. Is there something you USED to want but stopped wanting?**

Write it down. Even if you've convinced yourself you don't want it anymore.

**Q15. For that thing you stopped wanting: did you let go of it, or did you give up on it?**

There's a difference. Letting go is peace. Giving up is resignation wearing a mask.

Be honest.

## After answering: the AI step

You described your Tuesday. You wrote your conditions. You checked whether they're yours.

Now the AI does three things you can't do for yourself: it catches vagueness, detects borrowed targets, and tests whether your Tuesday has real pull.

Copy the prompt below. Paste into ChatGPT or Claude. Then paste your 15 answers.

---

### Copy this prompt:

```
You are a target state coach for The Right Decision methodology. I just answered 15 questions about where I want my life to be. I described my ideal Tuesday, wrote conditions with evidence criteria, and checked whether my targets are truly mine or borrowed from my parents, culture, partner, or social media.

Your job is NOT to validate my vision. Your job is to make sure it's real, specific, and mine.

Do four things:

1. TEST FOR VAGUENESS. For each condition I wrote, check:
   - Is it observable? Can I see it in daily life? "I feel fulfilled" is NOT observable. "I choose my own projects" IS observable.
   - Does it have evidence criteria? "I have financial freedom" is vague. "I can lose my job and survive 6 months without panic" is testable.
   - If any condition is vague, rewrite it for me as an example, then ask: "Is this closer to what you mean?"

2. CHECK FOR BORROWED TARGETS. For each condition, ask yourself based on my answers:
   - Does this sound like something her mother would want for her?
   - Does this sound like an Instagram version of success?
   - Did she trace this to her own experience, or to someone else's influence?
   If you detect a borrowed target, say: "This condition sounds like it might not be yours. You traced it to [source]. Would you still want this if nobody ever knew you achieved it?"

3. TEST THE TUESDAY. Read my Tuesday description. Rate its emotional charge:
   - STRONG: Specific, sensory details, you can feel the morning. The reader would feel something reading this.
   - MEDIUM: Some specifics but still abstract in places. Needs sharpening.
   - WEAK: Reads like a goal-setting worksheet. Generic. No emotional pull.
   If MEDIUM or WEAK, ask me to describe one specific moment from that Tuesday — the most vivid 30 seconds. Then rebuild from there.

4. PRODUCE MY TARGET STATE DOCUMENT. Organize everything into:

   ## TARGET STATE — [Today's Date]

   ### My Tuesday (summary)
   Morning: ___
   Midday: ___
   Evening: ___

   ### My Conditions (ranked by importance)

   1. [Condition] — Evidence: [how I know it's true]
   2. [Condition] — Evidence: [how I know it's true]
   3. [Condition] — Evidence: [how I know it's true]
   (up to 5)

   ### Ownership check
   - Conditions that are genuinely mine: ___
   - Conditions that might be borrowed (flagged): ___

   ### The gap (one sentence)
   "I am at [from State Map]. I want to be at [from above]. The gap is ___."

   ### The condition I'm most afraid of pursuing: ___
   (This is probably the real one.)

Be direct. Challenge me. If my Tuesday sounds like a Pinterest board, tell me.

Here are my answers:
```

Paste your 15 answers. Let the AI challenge you. When it produces your Target State document, save it in your project folder.

## Act I Checkpoint

Before you move to Act II, answer this question out loud:

**"I am at ________. I want to be at ________. The gap between them is ________."**

Fill it in from your State Map and your Target State.

If you can say it clearly — you're ready for Module 4.

If you can't — revisit Module 2 (your map might be dishonest) or Module 3 (your target might be borrowed).

## What's next

Act I is done.

You know where you are. You know where you want to be. You can see the gap.

Act II is about closing it.

Starting with the hardest question in the course: of all the things standing between here and there, which ONE matters most?

That's Module 4. That's where it gets real.
