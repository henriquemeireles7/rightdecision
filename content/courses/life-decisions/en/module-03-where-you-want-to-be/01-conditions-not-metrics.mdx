# Conditions, Not Metrics

## She hit $10K/month and still checked her bank account before buying groceries

I know a woman who made $10K/month her goal. She wrote it on a sticky note. Put it on her bathroom mirror. Visualized it every morning.

She hit it in 11 months.

And she still checked her bank account before buying groceries.

Why?

Because $10K/month with $8,500 in expenses, two credit cards, and no savings is not financial freedom. It's the same anxiety at a higher salary.

The number was never the point. The FEELING was the point. The condition she actually wanted was: "I don't worry about money when I buy things I need."

$10K didn't produce that. It could have, with different expenses. But she targeted the number instead of the condition, and the number lied to her.

## The difference between a metric and a condition

A **metric** is a number.

$10K/month. 50K followers. Size 6. 7 hours of sleep.

A **condition** is a state of life.

"I choose how I spend my Tuesdays." "I don't dread Monday." "I wake up without a knot in my stomach."

Metrics can help you get to conditions. But the metric is the checkpoint, not the destination.

**When you target the metric directly, you often achieve the number and miss the life.**

Here's what this looks like across different areas:

| The metric you chase | The condition you actually want |
|---|---|
| $10K/month | I don't worry about money when buying what I need |
| 50K followers | People I respect reach out to ME |
| Size 6 | I have energy to play with my kids after work |
| Married by 35 | I'm with someone who actually knows me |
| VP title | I choose my projects, not the other way around |

The metric might get you there. Or it might not.

50K followers who don't respect you isn't authority. A VP title you hate isn't freedom. A marriage by deadline isn't partnership.

**Target the condition. Use the metric as a checkpoint along the way.**

## How to describe a condition

A good condition has three qualities:

**1. You can observe it.**

Not "I feel confident." That's internal and unmeasurable.

Instead: "I walk into meetings and speak first instead of waiting to be asked." You can see that. You can check whether it's happening.

**2. You can test it.**

Not "I feel financially secure." How would you know?

Instead: "I can lose my job and survive for 6 months without panic." There's a specific test: do you have 6 months of expenses saved?

**3. It's yours.**

Not your mother's version of success. Not your culture's checklist. Not what Instagram shows you.

Yours.

How do you know? If nobody would ever see or know about it, would you still want it?

If yes, it's yours. If no, it's performance.

## The sticky note on the bathroom mirror

I'm not against goals. I'm not against numbers.

But the sticky note should say the condition, not the metric.

Not: "$10K/month."

Instead: "I don't check my bank account before buying groceries."

Not: "Lose 20 lbs."

Instead: "I have energy after 3pm."

The condition keeps you honest. The metric can lie to you. You can hit the number and miss the point.

The condition can't lie. Either you have energy after 3pm or you don't.
