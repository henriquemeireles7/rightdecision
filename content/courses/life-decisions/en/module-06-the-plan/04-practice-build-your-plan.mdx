# Practice: Build Your Plan

## The sixth document. One page. Everything you need to start tomorrow.

This is the last document before you start doing the thing.

After this, you have everything: where you are, where you want to be, what's in the way, the decision, and now the plan. Six documents in your folder. A complete picture.

The plan fits on one page. If it doesn't fit on one page, it's too complicated.

## The questions

Twelve questions. This is the most practical class in the course.

### Part 1: Objectives (Questions 1-3)

**Q1. What are the 3-5 measurable outcomes that make your decision real?**

A bad answer: "Feel better about my career."

A good answer:
1. 3 job applications submitted (metric: confirmation emails)
2. 2 mock interviews completed (metric: done or not done)
3. Salary negotiation above $6,000/month (metric: offer letter)

Each objective has a number. If it doesn't have a number, it's not an objective.

**Q2. For each objective, what's the deadline?**

Not "this quarter." A date.

**Q3. How will you know when each objective is complete?**

What's the evidence? An email? A bank statement? A conversation that happened?

### Part 2: Tasks (Questions 4-8)

**Q4. List every task needed to achieve your objectives. In order.**

Start each one with a verb: call, write, send, schedule, apply, register, open, ask.

**Q5. For each task, can you finish it in under 2 hours?**

If no, break it down further.

"Redo my entire resume" → Break into: "Rewrite summary section" (45 min), "Update work experience" (45 min), "Send to Julia for feedback" (10 min).

**Q6. For each task, assign a specific day.**

Not "this week." Tuesday. Wednesday. Saturday.

**Q7. What is the FIRST task?**

The one you do tomorrow. Or tonight.

Write it clearly: "Tomorrow [day], I will [task] at [time] for [duration]."

A bad answer: "I'll start working on things this week."

A good answer: "Tomorrow (Tuesday), I will list 10 target companies at my kitchen table after dinner. 30 minutes."

**Q8. Do any tasks depend on another task being done first?**

Map the dependencies. You can't apply to companies before you list them. You can't do a mock interview before you have a resume.

Draw the sequence. Which comes first? Which can happen in parallel?

### Part 3: Habits (Questions 9-11)

**Q9. What is the ONE habit that supports your decision?**

Formula: "After I [existing behavior], I will [new behavior] for [minimum duration]."

A bad answer: "I'll try to exercise more." (No trigger. No duration. Not a habit.)

A good answer: "After my morning coffee, I will spend 30 minutes on job search activities."

**Q10. What is the trigger?**

Name the existing behavior specifically. After coffee. After parking at work. After closing the laptop. After brushing teeth.

**Q11. What's the minimum viable version?**

On your worst day — exhausted, stressed, sick — what's the smallest version you'd still do?

Maria's minimum: "Open the job search tab and look at one listing." Two minutes. Even on the worst day.

### Part 4: The one-page plan (Question 12)

**Q12. Write your entire plan on one page.**

Format:

```
MY PLAN — [Date]

DECISION: "I will ___ by ___."

OBJECTIVES:
1. ___ (metric: ___) by [date]
2. ___ (metric: ___) by [date]
3. ___ (metric: ___) by [date]

TASKS (in order):
□ [Task 1] — [day]
□ [Task 2] — [day]
□ [Task 3] — [day]
□ [Task 4] — [day]
□ [Task 5] — [day]
...

HABIT:
"After I ___, I will ___ for at least ___."
Minimum viable version: ___

FIRST TASK: Tomorrow, [day], I will ___ at [time].
```

If it's longer than one page, cut something. Plans that feel heavy don't get executed. Plans that fit on one page get taped to the fridge.

## After answering: the AI step

The AI checks three things: Are your tasks small enough? Are your dependencies mapped? Is your first task identified and scheduled?
