# Decisions Are Not Goals

## Camila's lawyer got a 14-minute phone call that three years of therapy couldn't produce

We talked about Camila in Module 1. She wanted a divorce. Spent three years in therapy understanding why her marriage wasn't working.

Then one Tuesday she called a lawyer. Fourteen minutes.

Let me show you what happened in those three years vs those 14 minutes.

**Years 1-3:** Camila understood her attachment patterns. She could explain why she chose Carlos. She knew her mother's marriage influenced her expectations. She had a full emotional vocabulary for her dissatisfaction.

**The 14 minutes:** She said to the lawyer: "I want to file for divorce. What do I need to do?"

The lawyer said: "Do you have a joint bank account? Do you own property together? Do you have children? I'll need these documents."

That's it. No feelings. No patterns. No childhood analysis.

Just: what are the facts, and what are the next steps?

**The therapy gave her understanding. The phone call gave her a decision.**

Both have value. But only one changed her address.

## What makes a decision different from everything else

You probably have a list in your head of things you "want." Let me show you the difference between what you've been carrying and what you actually need.

| What you've been saying | What it is | What the decision would be |
|---|---|---|
| "I want to change careers" | A wish | "I will apply to 5 jobs in my target field by the 15th" |
| "I should save more money" | A thought | "I will set up a $300 automatic transfer every payday starting this Friday" |
| "I need to have the conversation with my mom" | An intention | "I will call her Saturday at 10am and say ___" |
| "I want to start my own thing" | A dream | "I will register the domain and buy the LLC this week" |

See the pattern?

**Wishes have no verb that happens on a specific day.**

**Decisions do.**

A wish is future tense. Comfortable. You can carry it for years.

A decision is present tense. Uncomfortable. It either happens or it doesn't.

That's why you have twenty wishes and zero decisions.

## The anatomy of a real decision

A real decision has exactly three components:

1. **A specific action.** Not "work on my career." Something a camera could record you doing. "Submit my resume to Company X."

2. **A date.** Not "soon." Not "when I'm ready." A day on a calendar. "By Friday."

3. **A witness.** Someone who knows. Decisions made silently die silently.

If it doesn't have all three, it's not a decision yet. It's still a wish wearing a decision's clothing.

*Look at your dominant constraint from Module 4. What's the decision that resolves it? Does it have an action, a date, and a witness?*

If you just went blank — that's normal. That's what the next two classes and the practice are for.
