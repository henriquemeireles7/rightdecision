# Say It Out Loud

## I stopped everything and went to the computer

I need to tell you something that happened to me.

I'd had multiple companies. Exits. Some worked, some didn't. Then I was almost unemployed for a year in the US. Stuck in that weird place where you're technically functional but nothing is moving.

I was doing all the right things. Therapy. Meditation. Books. Morning routines. Planning sessions. I had all the tools.

Nothing was moving.

Then one day I just... stopped.

I stopped blaming. I stopped meditating. I stopped reading self-help books. I stopped optimizing my morning routine. I stopped all of it.

I went to the computer and started doing things that made me excited.

That's it.

There was no breakthrough moment. No dramatic insight. I didn't finish a book and have an epiphany. I just woke up one morning completely fed up with preparing and started doing.

The first few days, I didn't even know what "the thing" was. I just did things. Wrote stuff. Built stuff. Talked to people about ideas.

After a few weeks, clarity arrived. Not before the action. After.

It was through doing that everything became clear. The theories I'd been juggling for months collapsed into one insight: **the decision was the thing.** Not the information. Not the motivation. The decision to do it, and then doing it.

## Why I'm telling you this now

Because of what I DIDN'T do.

I didn't tell anyone. For weeks. I just quietly started doing stuff. Didn't announce it. Didn't post about it. Didn't tell my wife what I was planning.

And every single day, there was a voice in my head saying: "This is stupid. Go back to the safe thing. Get a job. Stop playing around."

The voice almost won. Multiple times.

You know what would have killed that voice earlier?

Saying it out loud. To one person. "I'm doing this. This is what I'm building. I'll tell you how it goes."

**Decisions made silently die silently.** That quiet voice in your head has infinite ammunition when there's no witness. Nobody to disappoint. Nobody who expects you to follow through. Nobody who'll ask "hey, how's that thing going?"

## The commitment act

A decision becomes real through three actions. In order:

### 1. Write it down

Not in your head. On paper or on a screen.

"I will _____ by _____."

The act of writing forces specificity. "I'll work on my career" becomes "I will apply to three marketing director positions by April 30th."

If you can't write it in one sentence with a verb and a date, it's not a decision yet.

### 2. Say it out loud

Read it. To yourself. In the mirror. In the shower. In your car.

This sounds silly. It's not.

Something happens when you hear your own voice say the words. The decision moves from your thoughts into the physical world. Sound waves that existed in reality, not just in your head.

If you can't say it without your voice catching — that's how you know it's real.

### 3. Tell someone

This is the one that scares people most.

Not everyone. One person. Someone who will actually check in later. Not someone who'll let you off the hook. Someone who'll ask in two weeks: "Did you do the thing?"

Not social media. Not a journal. A human being who will look you in the eye and ask.

**The witness creates accountability.** The silent decision is between you and your own excuses. The witnessed decision has another human being in the loop. Excuses don't work as well when someone else is listening.

## What happens when you can't say it

Sometimes you try to say your decision out loud and you can't.

Your throat closes. You change the words. You soften it. "I'm thinking about maybe..." instead of "I will..."

That's information too.

It means one of two things:

**Either the decision isn't yours** — you're making it for someone else's reasons, and your body knows it. Go back to Module 3 and check.

**Or the decision is absolutely yours** — and it's so real and so scary that your body is trying to protect you from the commitment. This is the one you NEED to say out loud.

The fear of saying it IS the signal that it matters.

*Try it right now. Say your decision out loud. "I will ___ by ___." What happens in your body?*
