# Practice: Your First Review

## The eighth document. Fifteen minutes that save you months.

By now you've been executing for at least a week. Maybe two. You have real data — not hypotheticals, not plans. RESULTS.

This review tells you whether to keep pushing, adjust, or backtrack.

## The questions

Five questions. The same five every week. By Month 3, you'll do this from memory in 10 minutes.

**Q1. Look at your State Map from Module 2. What has ACTUALLY changed?**

Not how you feel. What changed. Facts and numbers.

A bad answer: "I feel more motivated." (Not a fact. Not measurable.)

A good answer: "Applied to 4 jobs (was 0). Got 1 interview scheduled. Sleep improved from 5 to 6.5 hours average. Saved $200 this month (was $0)."

Update your State Map with the new numbers.

**Q2. Are you closer to your Target State than last Sunday?**

Evidence, not feeling.

A bad answer: "I think so." (Evidence?)

A good answer: "Yes. I have an interview scheduled, which means I'm one step closer to choosing my own projects (Target Condition #1). My Sunday dread has decreased from 8/10 to 5/10."

**Q3. Is the dominant constraint still the right one?**

In most weeks, the answer is yes. But check:

- Did a new obstacle emerge that's bigger?
- Did your situation change in a way that makes a different constraint more urgent?
- Is the original constraint partially resolved, revealing a deeper one?

A bad answer: "I don't know." (Look at the data. What does it say?)

A good answer: "Yes, career is still the bottleneck. The interview is evidence that this path is working."

**Q4. Are my daily tasks moving the needle?**

Not "am I doing the tasks." Are they producing results?

A bad answer: "I'm doing everything on the list." (That's activity, not results.)

A good answer: "The applications are producing responses. But my habit of 30 minutes after coffee is drifting — I've been scrolling job sites instead of actively applying. Need to tighten the habit to 'apply to one company' instead of 'browse listings.'"

**Q5. Has anything major happened that changes my initial or end state?**

Job loss. Breakup. Health event. Unexpected opportunity. New information.

Most weeks: "No major changes."

If yes: describe what changed and whether it affects the constraint.

## After answering: the AI step

The AI reads your review and recommends one of three actions: keep going, adjust, or backtrack.

---

### Copy this prompt:

```
You are a weekly review coach for The Right Decision methodology. I just completed my weekly 15-minute review after [N] weeks of execution.

Your job is to analyze my progress and recommend ONE action: keep going, adjust, or backtrack.

Do four things:

1. COMPARE MY STATE. Look at my original State Map numbers and my updated numbers from today. Calculate the actual delta. What moved? What didn't?

2. CHECK THE DIRECTION. Am I getting closer to my Target State? Look for evidence, not feelings. If I said "I feel better" without data, push for specifics.

3. DIAGNOSE THE EXECUTION. Are my daily tasks producing results, or am I busy but not progressing?
   - If results exist: "Your tasks are working. Keep the same rhythm."
   - If no results after 3+ weeks: "Something isn't connecting. Let's look at whether it's the tasks (wrong actions), the constraint (wrong problem), or the decision (wrong commitment)."

4. GIVE YOUR RECOMMENDATION:

   **KEEP GOING** — The data shows progress. Same plan, same rhythm, next week.

   **ADJUST** — The direction is right but the tactics need tweaking. Recommend specific changes to the tasks or habit. Don't change the decision.

   **BACKTRACK** — Something fundamental isn't working. Recommend which module to revisit:
   - Module 6 (tasks are wrong)
   - Module 4 (constraint might be wrong)
   - Module 5 (decision might need revision)

Then produce my Weekly Review Document:

## WEEKLY REVIEW — [Date] (Week [N])

**State Map changes this week:**
- [Domain]: [old number] → [new number]
- [Domain]: [old number] → [new number]

**Closer to Target State?** [Yes/No + evidence]
**Dominant constraint still correct?** [Yes/No + reasoning]
**Tasks producing results?** [Yes/No + what's working, what's not]
**Major life changes?** [None / Description]

**RECOMMENDATION:** [Keep Going / Adjust / Backtrack]
**Specific next step:** [What to do differently this week, or "same plan"]

Be honest. If I'm deluding myself about progress, say so. If the data supports my optimism, confirm it.

Here are my answers:
```
