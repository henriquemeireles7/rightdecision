# Hard vs Wrong

## Two weeks in and nothing has changed. Do you quit or push through?

This is the question that kills most plans.

You've been doing the tasks. Following the habit. Showing up. But your State Map looks the same. The constraint hasn't budged. The Tuesday feels just as far away.

Two possible explanations:

1. **It's hard but working.** The progress is invisible. Seeds underground. You keep going.

2. **It's not working.** The plan is wrong. The constraint is wrong. Something needs to change.

**These two situations feel identical from the inside.** Both feel like frustration. Both feel like "nothing is happening." Both make you want to quit.

But the correct response is completely opposite:

- If it's hard but working → **persist**
- If it's not working → **backtrack**

So how do you tell the difference?

## Three signals that it's hard but working

**1. Your state map has small changes.**

Not dramatic ones. Small ones. Maria at Week 2: she'd applied to 5 companies. Zero offers yet. Looks like nothing happened.

But: 5 applications sent (was 0). 2 responses (was 0). Resume updated (was outdated). Sleep improved slightly.

Those small changes are roots growing underground. The tree isn't visible yet. But the roots are.

**2. The fear hasn't decreased.**

Counterintuitive. If you're doing something meaningful, the fear stays. It might even increase. You're getting closer to the real change. The stakes are getting higher.

If the fear is GROWING, you're probably on the right track. You're approaching the constraint, not avoiding it.

**3. You're uncomfortable but not confused.**

You know what you're doing. You know why. You just hate how it feels. The path is clear, even if the walk is painful.

This is "hard but working." Push through.

## Three signals that it's not working

**1. Your state map hasn't moved at all after 3+ weeks.**

Small changes count. Zero changes after three weeks of consistent execution is a signal.

Not after one week. One week is noise. But three weeks of daily effort with zero measurable movement means something is off.

**2. You're confused about what you're doing.**

Not scared. Confused. You wake up and don't know which task matters. The plan feels arbitrary. You're going through the motions but the connection between today's task and your Tuesday is unclear.

Confusion means the decomposition might be wrong. The tasks might not actually lead to the objectives.

**3. You're executing perfectly and feeling worse.**

This is the sneakiest signal.

Everything is going according to plan. You're checking boxes. But the discomfort from Module 1 is increasing, not decreasing. You feel MORE stuck, not less.

This usually means you're working on a symptom, not the constraint. Your body is telling you: this isn't it.

## What to do when it's not working

**Go back. But not all the way.**

- Tasks feel wrong? → Go back to **Module 6** (The Plan). Rebuild the tasks. Same decision, better decomposition.

- Constraint feels wrong? → Go back to **Module 4**. Reassess which obstacle is actually the bottleneck. Your situation may have changed.

- Decision feels wrong? → Go back to **Module 5**. This is rare, but it happens. Sometimes you committed to the wrong thing.

You DON'T go back to Module 1. Your awareness and state map are still valid. You don't restart the whole course. You just rewind to where the error is.

**Think of it like a GPS.** You took a wrong turn. The GPS doesn't send you back to the beginning of the trip. It reroutes from where you are.

*Look at your last few weeks. Are you hard-but-working, or not-working? What's your evidence?*
