# The Five Domains

## Maria scored 18 out of 50. She thought she was at 35.

After Maria wrote her honest state map, I asked her to rate each area of her life from 1 to 10.

She expected to land somewhere around 35. "I'm doing fine overall, just stuck in a couple areas."

She scored 18.

Career: 3. Relationship: 5. Health: 4. Finances: 3. Emotional: 3.

She stared at the number for a long time. Then she said something I'll never forget:

"I didn't realize how much I was lying to myself until I saw it as a number."

That's what this class does. We're going to turn your life into numbers. Not because numbers capture everything. But because numbers are harder to lie about than stories.

## Domain 1: Money

The one people lie about most.

**What to write down:**
- How much you earn per month after taxes
- How much you actually spend (check your bank app, not your memory)
- How much you have saved right now
- How much you owe and at what interest rate
- How much you spent on self-development last year

**What honest looks like:**

Not "I'm doing okay."

"I earn $4,200. I spend $3,800. I have $800 saved. I owe $12K at 6.8%. I spent $1,400 on courses that didn't change anything."

If your bank app says $312 and you're writing "doing okay," that's a story, not a map.

**Rate it 1-10.** Not relative to other people. Relative to the life you actually want.

## Domain 2: Career

Where you spend most of your waking hours. And often the domain you optimize AROUND instead of changing.

**What to write down:**
- Your role, how long you've been in it
- Three specific things you like about your work
- Three specific things you hate
- When you last updated your resume or looked at alternatives
- How Sunday night feels when you think about Monday

**What honest looks like:**

Not "I'm at a good company."

"I've been in the same role for 3 years. Passed over twice. My manager takes credit for my work. I haven't applied anywhere because the thought of interviewing makes me anxious."

**Rate it 1-10.**

## Domain 3: Relationships

The one people skip fastest. "It's fine."

If you said "fine" fast, that's the one.

**What to write down:**
- If you have a partner: when was your last real conversation? Not logistics. A real one.
- How many friends know the actual you? Not the curated version.
- Is there a family conversation you've been avoiding?
- How often do you feel genuinely lonely? Daily? Weekly? Rarely?

**What honest looks like:**

Not "My relationship is stable."

"We haven't talked about anything real in 4 months. I haven't told him I'm unhappy because I'm afraid of what happens next."

**Rate it 1-10.**

## Domain 4: Health

The domain your body uses to send signals about unmade decisions. We covered this in the last module.

**What to write down:**
- Hours of sleep per night and quality
- Your energy level at 3pm on a normal weekday (1-10)
- Weight change in the last year
- How often you exercise and what kind
- Anything you use to cope: alcohol, caffeine, scrolling, cannabis, medication — what and how often

No judgment. Just facts.

**What honest looks like:**

Not "I'm healthy but could exercise more."

"I gained 15 lbs this year. I sleep 5 hours most nights. I drink wine 3 times a week to fall asleep. I can't remember the last time I felt genuinely energized."

**Rate it 1-10.**

## Domain 5: How you actually feel

Not your feelings right now. Your baseline. The emotional state you default to when nothing specific is happening.

**What to write down:**
- When you're alone and undistracted, what's the default emotion?
- How often do you cry unexpectedly?
- What are you pretending is fine?
- One sentence: how do you actually feel about your life right now?

**What honest looks like:**

Not "I'm mostly happy."

"I feel like I'm running but going nowhere. I'm exhausted in a way that sleep doesn't fix. I've shrunk my dreams to fit my comfort zone and I'm pretending that's wisdom."

**Rate it 1-10.**

## Your total score

Add up your five ratings. Maximum is 50.

| Score | What it means |
|---|---|
| **40-50** | You're fine-tuning, not stuck. Your constraint is specific. |
| **25-39** | You're stuck in 1-2 areas. The methodology is built for this. |
| **15-24** | Multiple areas are affected. The real constraint is probably hiding behind the obvious ones. |
| **Below 15** | Consider professional support alongside this course, not instead of it. |

Maria expected 35. She got 18. The gap between what she thought and what was true was the most valuable thing she'd discovered in two years of self-help.

**What's your gap?**
