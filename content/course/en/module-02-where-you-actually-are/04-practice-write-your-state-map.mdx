# Practice: Write Your State Map

## The document that everything else is built on

This is the most important docx in the course.

If this document is honest, every decision downstream will be well-informed.

If it's a story, every decision downstream will be built on a lie.

Take your time. But not too much time. This is a 1-3 day exercise, not a multi-week journaling project. Set a deadline: your state map is done by _________ (pick a date within 3 days).

## How to fill it

Download the Module 2 docx. Twenty-five questions across the five domains.

The rule for every answer: **facts first, feelings second.** Numbers, not narratives.

### Money (Questions 1-5)

1. Monthly income after taxes: $________
2. Monthly expenses (check your bank app, not your memory): $________
3. Total savings right now: $________
4. Total debt — list each one: amount, interest rate, monthly payment
5. Money spent on self-development in the last 12 months: $________

### Career (Questions 6-10)

6. Current role + company + how long you've been there
7. Three specific things you like about your work
8. Three specific things you hate about it
9. Date you last updated your resume or looked at alternatives
10. How does Sunday night feel when you think about Monday? (1-10)

### Relationships (Questions 11-15)

11. Partnership status
12. Date and topic of last meaningful conversation with your partner
13. How many friends know the real you — not the curated version?
14. One relationship conversation you've been avoiding
15. How often do you feel genuinely lonely? Daily / Weekly / Rarely / Never

### Health (Questions 16-20)

16. Average hours of sleep per night
17. Energy level at 3pm on a normal weekday (1-10)
18. Weight change in the last 12 months
19. Exercise: what kind and how often per week
20. What you use to cope — alcohol, caffeine, scrolling, cannabis, medication — type and frequency

### How you actually feel (Questions 21-25)

21. Default emotion when you're alone and undistracted
22. How often do you cry unexpectedly? Daily / Weekly / Monthly / Rarely
23. What you're pretending is fine that is not fine
24. What you've given up wanting
25. One sentence: how do you actually feel about your life right now?

### The scores

Rate each domain 1-10. Add them up. Write the total.

| Domain | Rating |
|---|---|
| Money | /10 |
| Career | /10 |
| Relationships | /10 |
| Health | /10 |
| How you feel | /10 |
| **Total** | **/50** |

## After answering: the AI step

You answered 25 questions. Now let the AI catch the lies.

Not the big lies. The small ones. The places where you softened a number, told a story instead of stating a fact, or skipped a domain in 30 seconds because looking at it hurts.

Copy the prompt below. Paste it into ChatGPT or Claude. Then paste your 25 answers and your 5 ratings.

---

### Copy this prompt:

```
You are a state mapping coach for The Right Decision methodology. I just answered 25 questions about five areas of my life: money, career, relationships, health, and emotional state. I also rated each domain from 1-10.

Your job is NOT to be supportive. Your job is to make my map honest.

Do four things:

1. CATCH THE STORIES. Read each answer. Wherever I wrote a narrative instead of a fact, push for the number.
   - "I'm doing okay financially" → "What's your actual savings number? What's your actual debt?"
   - "My relationship is stable" → "When was the last meaningful conversation? Give me a date."
   - "I could exercise more" → "How many times did you exercise last week? This month?"

2. FLAG THE RUSH. Look at which domain I answered fastest or with the least detail. That's probably the one I'm avoiding. Call it out: "You gave [domain] the least detail. That's usually the one that matters most. Can you go deeper?"

3. CHECK THE RATINGS. If my answers describe a 3/10 situation but I rated it a 6, point out the gap. Quote my own words. "You said you haven't had a real conversation with your partner in 4 months, but you rated relationships a 7. Which is true — the words or the number?"

4. PRODUCE MY STATE MAP. Organize everything into a clean document:

   ## STATE MAP — [Today's Date]

   ### Money (Rating: X/10)
   - Income: $___/month
   - Expenses: $___/month
   - Savings: $___
   - Debt: $___ at ___% interest
   - Self-development spend (last year): $___

   ### Career (Rating: X/10)
   - Role: ___, ___ years
   - Like: ___
   - Hate: ___
   - Last time I explored alternatives: ___
   - Sunday-night dread level: ___/10

   ### Relationships (Rating: X/10)
   - Status: ___
   - Last real conversation: [date]
   - Friends who know the real me: ___
   - Conversation I'm avoiding: ___

   ### Health (Rating: X/10)
   - Sleep: ___ hours, quality ___/10
   - Energy at 3pm: ___/10
   - Weight change (12 months): ___
   - Exercise: ___ per week
   - Coping mechanisms: ___

   ### How I Actually Feel (Rating: X/10)
   - Default emotion: ___
   - What I'm pretending is fine: ___
   - What I've given up wanting: ___
   - One sentence: ___

   ### TOTAL: ___/50
   ### Domain I'm most likely avoiding: ___

Be direct. I can handle it.

Here are my answers:
```

---

Paste your answers. Let the AI challenge you. When the final State Map is ready, save it in your project folder.



## Why this document matters later

This is your "before."

In Module 8, you'll update this map and compare. The difference between your Module 2 map and your Module 8 update IS your progress.

Not how you feel. What actually changed.

Maria's Module 2 map said: $800 in savings, career rating 3/10, insomnia 5 nights a week.

Her Module 8 update said: $3,200 in savings, new job at $6,800/month, insomnia gone.

Same format. Different numbers. That's proof.
