# The Tuesday Test

## Why Tuesday? Because it's too boring to lie about.

Not Monday. Monday is loaded with work dread. Your answers would be about escaping your current job, not about the life you want.

Not Friday. Friday is colored by weekend excitement. You'd describe a vacation, not a life.

Tuesday is the most ordinary day of the week. Nothing special happens. Nobody posts "Happy Tuesday!" on Instagram.

That's why it's perfect.

**If you can design a Tuesday you'd genuinely love to live, you've found your target state.**

## Close your eyes and describe it

Imagine your dominant constraint is resolved. The decision you've been avoiding? Made. Executed. Behind you.

You're living in the condition you actually want.

Now describe a specific Tuesday.

### Morning

- What time do you wake up? Alarm or naturally?
- What's the first thing you do? (Not "gratitude practice." What do you *actually* do?)
- Who's next to you? Or are you alone? Do you prefer it?
- What do you eat? Where?

### Midday

- What are you working on? Did you choose it?
- Who are you talking to? About what?
- Where are you? Home? Office? Coffee shop? Another country?

### Afternoon

- How's your energy at 3pm? Compare to the number you wrote in Module 2.
- What's the thing you DON'T have to do anymore?
- What meeting, obligation, or commute is absent from your day?

### Evening

- What do you do when work ends?
- Who are you having dinner with? What do you talk about?
- When you go to bed, what's in your chest? Not "grateful." Actually.

## Why this works

The Tuesday Test does three things.

**It makes the abstract concrete.**

"Financial freedom" is a concept. You can think about it forever and never know if you've achieved it.

"I don't check my bank account before buying groceries and I go to bed without calculating whether I can afford next month."

That's a Tuesday. You can feel it.

**It reveals what you ACTUALLY want.**

Some people describe a Tuesday full of hustle. Then they pause. Wait. Actually... I want a slow morning. Time to read. A walk before noon.

The Tuesday you want to live tells you more about your target than any goal-setting exercise.

**It creates an emotional anchor.**

When you can see the Tuesday, you can feel it. That feeling is the fuel for the decision in Module 5.

You're not deciding toward a number. You're deciding toward a morning, an afternoon, an evening that you can almost taste.

## The test that tells you if it's real

After you describe your Tuesday, read it out loud. To yourself. In the mirror if you're brave.

Pay attention to your body.

If your throat tightens a little — it's yours.

If your eyes sting — it's yours.

If you feel impatient, like "I want this NOW" — it's yours.

If you feel scared, like "can I really have this?" — it's yours.

**If you feel nothing — no pull, no tension, no longing — it's not yours.** It's someone else's Tuesday. Start over.

Maria described her Tuesday: she wakes up at 7:30 without an alarm. She works on a marketing campaign she chose, for a product she believes in. At 3pm she has energy. She doesn't dread Sunday nights.

When she read it out loud, she cried.

Not from sadness. From the distance between where she was and where she wanted to be.

That distance is the gap. And the next class is about making sure you're aiming at YOUR target, not someone else's.
