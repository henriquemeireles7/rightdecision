## The Premise

Most companies add AI to existing workflows. We do the opposite: we design workflows for AI agents first, then make them accessible to humans.

This isn't about replacing humans. It's about recognizing that the bottleneck in most knowledge work isn't thinking — it's execution. An agent that can execute a well-defined workflow frees the human to focus on judgment calls.

## What Agent-First Looks Like

Every workflow at The Right Decision follows this pattern:

1. **Define the decision** — what needs to be decided, by whom, with what constraints
2. **Design for agents** — what steps can be fully automated, what needs human judgment
3. **Build the automation** — agents handle the execution, humans handle the exceptions
4. **Measure and iterate** — the agent gets better, the human focuses on fewer decisions

## Why This Matters

A solo founder with agents can ship what used to take a team of 10. Not because the agents are smarter than 10 people, but because they never sleep, never context-switch, and never forget the checklist.

The limiting factor shifts from "how many people can I hire" to "how well can I define my workflows."
