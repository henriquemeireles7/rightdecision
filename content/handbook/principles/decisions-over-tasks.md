## The Problem with Task Lists

Task lists feel productive. You check things off. The list gets shorter. But the list never asks: "Is this the right thing to work on?"

Most task systems optimize for throughput. How many tasks did you complete? That's the wrong metric. The right metric is: how many good decisions did you make?

## How We Think About It

Every piece of work at The Right Decision starts with a decision, not a task:

- **What problem are we solving?** (Not: what feature are we building)
- **Who does this help?** (Not: who assigned this ticket)
- **What's the smallest thing we can ship to learn?** (Not: what's the full spec)

## The Decision Tree

We use a simple hierarchy:

1. **Strategic decisions** — what to build, who to serve, where to invest
2. **Design decisions** — how it works, what it looks like, how it feels
3. **Implementation decisions** — technical choices that serve the design
4. **Execution** — the actual building (this is where tasks live)

Tasks are the output of good decisions. Starting with tasks is starting at the wrong end.
