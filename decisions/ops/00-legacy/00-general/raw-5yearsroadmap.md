## Problem Statement

The current roadmap (decisions/roadmap.md) is a task list, not a vision. It tracks what to build next but doesn't answer: where are we aiming? What's the 5-year arc? How does every near-term decision serve the long-term vision? The founder wants a roadmap that generates thousands of work items organized around a clear mission — building a vertical AI company that helps people make decisions and act on them, with its own model and hardware.

## Demand Evidence

The founder IS the ICP. 3x entrepreneur, $30M+ revenue, went through depression after an exit. Tried every solution on the market (therapy, meditation, sacred medicines, workshops, manifestation, consciousness work, relationship changes) — everything except deciding. Coached 400+ businesses. Went viral on LinkedIn, Quora, Facebook, Instagram. His wife Indy is the second customer and content partner. They are dogfooding both products daily.

Key quote: "I am building this because I wish I had this product for me a few years ago. If people like it, awesome it means I will make loads of money. If they don't, it means I just need to keep iterating."

## Status Quo

People try everything except deciding. The self-help industry ($13B market) profits from the dependency loop: meditations, medications, workshops, healing, sacred medicines, spiritual practices, energy work, manifestation, courses, books. Zero AI-native companies in the space. The gap between "enterprise decision intelligence" ($50B market) and "self-help" is wide open. Nobody is building a vertical AI company around the STRUCTURE of human decision-making.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Target:** "The Stuck Achiever" — woman 30-50 who has done the work but is stuck. Disguises indecision as research. And "The Overthinker" — man 25-40, consumes content but never starts.

**Narrowest wedge:** The free Life Decisions course. One person through the decision methodology, transformed on the other side. This is the proof point everything else builds on.

## Premises

1. **"Deciding is the primitive"** — every meaningful life/business change starts with a decision, not information, motivation, or a plan. The methodology teaches the STRUCTURE of deciding.
2. **GTM is infobusiness, real company is vertical AI** — courses + skills at $197/$1,997 fund the real company: a vertical AI company that collects structured decision-making data and eventually trains its own model + builds hardware.
3. **Life Decisions (B2C) is proof + data; Business Decisions (B2B) is scale + revenue** — both feed the same data flywheel.
4. **Free course is the narrowest wedge** — one person through the methodology, transformed.
5. **5-phase arc is non-negotiable: GTM → AI platform → Own model → Own hardware** — full stack ownership is the vision. Each phase is independently valuable but the arc is the point.

## Cross-Model Perspective

Codex steelmanned: "This is a decision-first company that uses an infobusiness wedge to capture intent, teach a repeatable decision protocol, and harvest high-signal decision data. The real product is a vertical decision engine that turns 'knowing' into 'doing.'"

Codex challenged Premise 5 (own model + hardware): argued commodity models may be sufficient if they produce great results with healthy unit economics. Founder defended the premise: "the whole point is owning the full stack." Codex's 48-hour build suggestion: a "Decision Record" MVP with guided d-meta → d-input → d-plan flow, signed decision statement, first 15-min task, and 7-day email follow-up loop with outcomes dashboard.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Phase-Gated Sequential
Strict 5-phase roadmap. Each phase completes before the next. Simple but rigid. Doesn't capture parallel product development. Risk of near-term decisions not being load-bearing for later phases. Completeness: 6/10.

### Approach B: Parallel Tracks + Phase Horizons
Two parallel product tracks (LD + BD) with 5 phase horizons. Every feature tagged with which phases it serves. Generates massive backlog. Each phase independently valuable. Completeness: 9/10.

### Approach C: Flywheel-First
Organize around the data flywheel (User decides → Data captured → Model improves → Better decisions → More users). Products are surfaces on the flywheel. Most aligned with vertical AI vision but risks premature optimization. Completeness: 7/10.

## Recommended Approach: B + C Combined

**Parallel Tracks organized around the Data Flywheel.**

Two product tracks (Life Decisions + Business Decisions) running in parallel across 5 phase horizons. Every initiative is evaluated by how much it accelerates the flywheel: **User decides → Structured data captured → Model improves → Better decisions → More users.**

## DECISION FRAMEWORK FOR NEAR-TERM CHOICES

Every feature decision should pass this test:

1. **Does it accelerate the flywheel?** (User decides → Data captured → Model improves → Better decisions → More users)
2. **Is it load-bearing for Phase 2+?** (Will this decision still serve us when we have our own model?)
3. **Does the data schema support it?** (Can we capture structured data from this feature?)
4. **Does it strengthen the anti-self-help positioning?** (Is this about action, not introspection?)
5. **Can we dogfood it?** (Will Henry + Indy use this themselves?)

If a feature passes all 5: build it now.
If it passes 3-4: build it, but note which tests it fails.
If it passes 1-2: defer it, revisit next quarter.

## Open Questions

1. **Data consent model:** How explicit must consent be for using decision data to train models? GDPR/CCPA implications. Need legal review before Phase 2.
2. **Skill distribution:** npm, git clone, or MCP server for Claude skills? Affects onboarding friction and update mechanism.
3. **Model architecture:** Fine-tune open model (Llama, Mistral) or train from scratch? Depends on data volume and specificity needs. Decision point at 1M records.
4. **Hardware timing:** When does own hardware become economically viable? Need inference cost modeling at scale.
