## 2. Customer + Problem

### Who she is

A woman, 30-50, who has done the work. She moved cities, changed jobs, built a career, tried therapy, read the books, did the courses. She is objectively ahead of her peers.

And she is stuck. Not at the beginning. Stuck at a higher level, where the problems are harder to name and the solutions are harder to find.

### What she actually does today

She consumes. She buys the $47 course on feminine energy. She reads a self-help book a month. She follows 15 Instagram coaches. She journals every morning. She meditates.

She is committing the third sin of decisions: **disguising indecision as research.** Reading one more book, taking one more course, having one more conversation, not to inform a decision, but to postpone it.

She is also committing the first sin: **solving the second-biggest constraint because the biggest one is scary.** She optimizes her morning routine while the real problem, the relationship or the career or the move, sits untouched.

### What it costs her

Every day she doesn't decide, she pays in three currencies:

**Time.** She has spent 2+ years thinking about the same decision. Those months produced more thinking, not clarity.

**Energy.** Unmade decisions run in the background like apps draining the battery. She is exhausted before the day starts. This is not burnout from overwork. This is burnout from indecision.

**Compounding opportunity cost.** The cost of the wrong decision is visible. The cost of no decision is invisible, and usually higher. Every day in the current state is a day not in the next state. If the next state is better, every day of delay is a day of the better life she is not living.

### Why existing solutions fail

The entire self-help industry optimizes for one thing: understanding. Understand your trauma. Understand your patterns. Understand your attachment style. Understand your inner child.

Understanding is an input to decisions, but most people use it as a substitute for decisions. They research endlessly, consume content obsessively, and call it progress. It is not progress. It is sophisticated procrastination.

- **Therapy** gives tools to understand the past. It does not tell you what to decide or when.
- **Life coaching** ($200-500/hour) is expensive and creates dependency on the coach's diagnosis.
- **Online courses** teach frameworks without applying them to HER specific dominant constraint.
- **Self-help books** are consumed, underlined, and forgotten. The atomic unit of change is the decision, not the insight.
- **Free AI (ChatGPT)** can help but has no methodology, no context memory, and no framework for identifying the dominant constraint.

## 3. Solution + Mechanism

### The core thesis

Every meaningful change in your life begins with a decision. Not with information. Not with motivation. Not with a plan. A decision.

You are not stuck because you lack resources, time, or luck. You are stuck because you are not making the right decisions, or you are making no decisions at all, which is the worst decision of all.

The Right Decision is not a single mythical choice that fixes everything. It is a discipline: identifying the dominant constraint, committing to resolve it, and reorganizing your life around that commitment.

### The mechanism: The Decision Cycle

**Step 1. Honest State Mapping.** Where are you right now? The accuracy of your decisions is capped by the accuracy of your self-assessment. The map must be honest or every downstream decision is built on a lie.

**Step 2. Target State Definition.** Where do you want to be? Define this as a condition of life, not a metric. "Freedom to choose how I spend my time" is a state. "$50K/month" is a metric that may or may not serve the state.

**Step 3. Dominant Constraint Identification.** What is the single biggest thing between your current state and your target state? Not the most visible. Not the most urgent. The one that, if resolved, would create the most forward movement. This is the hardest step. The dominant constraint is usually the one you are avoiding.

**Step 4. Decision Commitment.** Based on the dominant constraint, what do you need to decide? Name it explicitly. Write it down. Make it concrete and irreversible enough that you cannot sleepwalk back to the default.

**Step 5. Decision Decomposition.** Break the decision into its execution structure: objectives (measurable outcomes), tasks (time-bound actions), and habits (recurring behaviors that sustain execution without willpower). Decisions without habits decay. Habits without decisions drift.

**Step 6. Execution with Sustainability.** Execute within a life you can sustain. Optimize across time, effort, and quality of life. Burnout is not a badge. It is a system failure.

**Step 7. Feedback and Re-evaluation.** Time changes all variables. The cycle repeats, triggered by signals: a constraint resolved, progress stalled, a major event reshuffled the variables.

### The seven angles (content pillars)

Each angle is a lens on the same primitive, the decision. Each becomes a content pillar, a course module hook, and a sales letter angle:

1. **The Decision as the Atomic Unit of Change.** "You don't have an information problem. You have a decision problem."
2. **The Decision as Diagnosis.** "You're making good decisions about the wrong problems."
3. **The Decision as Priority Declaration.** "If your decision doesn't rearrange your life, it wasn't a decision. It was a wish."
4. **The Decision as Risk Acceptance.** "If you're waiting until it feels safe, you'll wait forever."
5. **The Decision as Temporal Act.** "The cost of the wrong decision is visible. The cost of no decision is invisible."
6. **The Decision as Ownership.** "No one else can make the right decision for your life."
7. **The Decision as System.** "The right decision isn't a moment. It's a muscle."

### BJ Fogg model applied

**MOTIVATION (the course):** 9 modules across 3 acts (See Clearly, Decide, Move), totaling ~23 hours over 3 months at 2 hours/week. Taught through Henry and Indy's stories. Each module maps to one phase of the methodology. The course does not teach understanding. It teaches the discipline of deciding. Each module ends with a practical class: a docx questionnaire (manual work first) + AI prompt (refinement second).

**ABILITY (the AI platform):** The AI runs the Decision Cycle for HER specific situation. It maps her state, identifies her dominant constraint, names the decision, and decomposes it into objectives, tasks, and habits. Same methodology, personalized to her variables.

**TRIGGER (daily prompts):** "Did you execute today's task?" Yes or no. The system becomes a decision execution partner, not a content library.

### The eight sins (what the product prevents)

1. Solving the second-biggest constraint because the biggest one is scary
2. Outsourcing the diagnosis
3. Disguising indecision as research
4. Deciding without decomposing
5. Decomposing without deciding
6. Copying someone else's decision
7. Optimizing for reversibility
8. Not deciding, and pretending that is not a decision

## 7. Retention + Year 2

### The tension

The Right Decision framework says: the system beats the moment. The product should embody this. It is not a course you consume and leave. It is a cycle you operate.

### Why she renews

Life is an infinite evolution path. There will always be new constraints. The loop never pauses.

She resolved the career constraint in Month 3 (Act III of the course). By Month 6, the relationship constraint surfaces. In Year 2, the financial freedom constraint. Each cycle through the loop is faster because she has done it before and the AI has her history.

The reward is not in the resolution. The reward is in the execution itself. "Every day you wake up and do the thing, you're already better than yesterday." This is why the product retains: it is not content to consume, it is a system to operate. The system compounds.

### Retention mechanics

1. **The loop IS retention:** New constraint → same methodology → faster cycle. The course is re-enterable at any module.
2. **Decision history:** Completed decisions build visible agency. The AI project folder shows every resolved constraint.
3. **Course updates:** New modules on specific domains (financial decisions, relationship decisions, career decisions)
4. **Price lock:** Renew at original price. New customers pay more.

### Churn prediction

Realistic: 50% Year 1 churn (typical for digital products). Target: below 40%.

The anti-dependency tension is resolved: the product does not create dependency on content or on the founders. It creates dependency on the SYSTEM — the cycle of diagnose → decide → decompose → execute → re-evaluate. The system is the value. Each pass through the loop builds skill that makes the next pass easier. The AI makes the system effortless.

🟡 *Confidence: hypothesis. No retention data, but the methodology's loop structure provides a clear retention mechanism.*

## 9. Competitive Positioning

### The positioning statement

The Right Decision is not self-help. Self-help gives you understanding. The Right Decision gives you a system for deciding accurately and executing consistently. For the price of one therapy session, you get a year of AI-powered decision clarity.

### What makes this different (honest)

**Today (weak):** The moat is the foundational framework (seven angles, eight sins, the Decision Cycle) and Henry + Indy's story. Copyable.

**In 6 months (medium):** The AI platform with user context. The AI that knows your dominant constraint, your state map, your decision history.

**In 12 months (strong):** The data. An AI that has decomposed 1,000 real dominant constraints knows which questions to ask. That pattern library is unique and compounds.

### The name advantage

"The Right Decision" (singular) IS the methodology. It forces the audience to confront: you cannot solve everything at once. You must identify the ONE constraint and make the ONE decision that matters most right now. The name teaches.
