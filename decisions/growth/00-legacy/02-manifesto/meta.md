# META-DOC: Manifesto

## Purpose
Define what Right Decision believes, how it sounds, and the exact words used to sell. This is BOTH the brand identity AND the copy bible. Every headline, email, ad, video hook, and landing page sentence traces back to this document.

## Scope
**This document IS:** Brand thesis + voice + sales copy source material. The words that sell.
**This document is NOT:** A course outline (doc #4). A landing page spec (doc #5). A VSL script (doc #6). Visual identity (that's DESIGN.md).
**Document hierarchy:** DESIGN.md governs visuals. Manifesto governs words. When building a page, read both. If voice/tone conflicts with visual tone, manifesto wins for copy, DESIGN.md wins for aesthetics.

## Primary reader
Content creators (Henry, Indy, future copywriters, AI agents writing ads/emails/social).

## Input documents
- `decisions/01-business-model/document.md` — one-liner, ICP, seven angles, eight sins, Decision Cycle, Hormozi value equation
- `decisions/01-business-model/input.md` — foundational framework (core thesis, mechanism, principles in the founder's raw voice)
- `DESIGN.md` — aesthetic direction (Ethereal Warmth) that copy tone must harmonize with

## Research summary
**Layer 1:** Direct response copy follows awareness stages (unaware → problem-aware → solution-aware → product-aware → most-aware). The manifesto must produce hooks for EACH stage.

**Layer 2:** In 2026, the self-development space is saturated with manifestos that all sound the same: empowering, feminine, healing-coded. Right Decision must sound DIFFERENT: direct, grounded, anti-woo. The aesthetic is calm authority (from DESIGN.md), not inspirational energy.

**Layer 3:** First principles: a manifesto for an infobusiness is not a brand statement. It's a SELL SYSTEM. Every section must produce usable copy. If a section can't generate at least 5 headlines, it doesn't belong.

**Adjacent wisdom:** Political speechwriting. The best political speech has: a clear enemy, a diagnosis of what's wrong, a mechanism for change, and a call to action. Right Decision's manifesto follows the same arc.

## Document-level failure modes
1. **Framework worship** — falls in love with its own terminology (31+ concepts). The customer doesn't care about your labels. She cares about her life.
2. **Sounds like every other coach** — "empowering women to live their best life." If it could be said by any competitor, delete it.
3. **Intellectual but not emotional** — the thesis is right but doesn't make anyone feel anything. Must include the customer's emotional register, not just the framework's logic.
4. **Beautiful but unusable** — read once, admired, never opened again. Every section must end with "use this when..." instructions.
5. **One voice for all contexts** — cold ad vs onboarding email vs post-purchase coaching need different registers, not one flat tone.

## Sections

### SECTION 1: Core Thesis (intellectual + emotional)
**Answers:** What does Right Decision believe, in one paragraph? Two versions: the intellectual thesis (for internal alignment) and the emotional thesis (for the customer).
**Done when:** Read the emotional version to 5 people in the target demo. If fewer than 4 react (agree or disagree), rewrite.
**Failure modes:**
- Too abstract: "Decisions matter" (no tension, no enemy)
- Too clever: "The decision is the primitive" (framework language, not customer language)
- Too long: If it's more than 3 sentences per version, you don't understand it yet
**Max length:** 1 paragraph each (intellectual + emotional)
**Confidence:** 🟢 validated (exists in input.md)

### SECTION 2: The Enemy + Positioning
**Answers:** What are we against? What are we replacing? Not just ideologically (self-help industry) but tactically (therapy, coaching, courses, free ChatGPT).
**Done when:** A content writer can look at ANY competitor and articulate in one sentence why Right Decision is different.
**Failure modes:**
- Enemy is too vague: "the system" vs naming specific alternatives
- Enemy is people instead of ideas: never attack therapists, attack the dependency model
- No tactical positioning: "we're against self-help" but can't explain why we're better than BetterUp
**Max length:** 1-2 pages
**Confidence:** 🟡 hypothesis

### SECTION 3: Voice of Customer
**Answers:** How does SHE describe her problem? In her words, not ours. What does she say to her friend at brunch? What does she type into Google at midnight?
**Done when:** A copywriter can read this section and write a cold Instagram ad without using any of our framework terminology.
**Failure modes:**
- Using our words instead of hers: "dominant constraint" vs "I know what I need to do but I just can't make myself do it"
- Only capturing the problem, not the emotional state (frustration, shame, exhaustion, hope)
- Too sanitized: real human language is messy, contradictory, and specific
**Max length:** 1-2 pages
**Confidence:** 🟡 hypothesis (informed by friends' conversations, not formal research)

### SECTION 4: Seven Angles + Hook Bank
**Answers:** The seven lenses on "the decision." For EACH angle: thesis, emotional hook (5-10 variations at different awareness levels), counter-argument, rebuttal.
**Done when:** A social media manager can open this section, pick an angle, and write a week's worth of content without asking anyone what to say.
**Failure modes:**
- Only one hook per angle (need 5-10 variations per awareness level)
- Hooks sound like the framework ("The decision as temporal act") instead of the customer ("Every day you don't decide, you're paying for it")
- No awareness-level tagging: a hook for someone who's never heard of you is different from a hook for someone who's been following for 3 months
**Max length:** 2-3 pages (biggest section)
**Confidence:** 🟢 validated (exists in input.md)

### SECTION 5: Eight Sins + Problem Hooks
**Answers:** The eight anti-patterns, each as a "are you doing this?" diagnostic content piece. Maps each sin to the corresponding angle it violates.
**Done when:** Each sin produces at least 3 content hooks. A sin→angle mapping prevents duplicate content.
**Failure modes:**
- Sounds judgmental instead of diagnostic: "you're doing it wrong" vs "here's the pattern that keeps you stuck"
- No mapping to angles: content team makes the same piece twice from different sections
**Max length:** 1-2 pages
**Confidence:** 🟢 validated (exists in input.md)

### SECTION 6: The Mechanism (customer version)
**Answers:** How does The Right Decision actually work? The Decision Cycle explained for a buyer, not an engineer.
**Done when:** Someone who's never seen the product can explain the 7-step cycle after reading this once.
**Failure modes:**
- Too technical: step names that sound like a consulting framework
- No "before and after" contrast: must show what life looks like BEFORE the cycle vs AFTER
- Doesn't differentiate from generic goal-setting
**Max length:** 1-2 pages
**Confidence:** 🟡 hypothesis

### SECTION 7: Voice + Tone (multi-register)
**Answers:** How does Right Decision sound? Three registers: cold (awareness-stage, empathetic), warm (consideration-stage, authoritative), post-purchase (coaching, direct).
**Done when:** A writer can take the same idea and write it in all three registers, and all three sound like Right Decision.
**Failure modes:**
- One flat tone for everything: cold ads sound like onboarding emails
- Tone contradicts DESIGN.md aesthetic: copy is aggressive but design is calm
- No "never say" list: must include words and phrases the brand never uses
**Max length:** 1-2 pages
**Confidence:** 🟡 hypothesis

### SECTION 8: Brand Vocabulary
**Answers:** Our proprietary terms, with customer-facing vs internal usage rules.
**Done when:** For each term: the term, plain-English equivalent, when to use it, where NOT to use it (e.g., "decision decomposition" = internal only, never in ads).
**Failure modes:**
- Glossary without usage rules (just a list that gets ignored)
- Too many terms: if the vocabulary has more than 12 entries, it's framework worship
**Max length:** 1 page (table format)
**Confidence:** 🟢 validated

### SECTION 9: The Promise + Objection Map
**Answers:** What transformation does the customer get? The one-sentence hero promise. Plus: the top 7 purchase objections with scripted responses.
**Done when:** The promise goes on the landing page hero. Each objection has a response usable in an FAQ, email, or DM.
**Failure modes:**
- Promise is features ("7 modules + AI") instead of transformation ("Finally make the decision you've been avoiding for 2 years")
- Objections are philosophical instead of practical: "What if decisions aren't the answer?" vs "What if I don't finish the course?"
**Max length:** 1-2 pages
**Confidence:** 🔴 guess (promise not tested with buyers)

### SECTION 10: Origin Story
**Answers:** Why does Right Decision exist? Henry and Indy's story. Not a biography. The specific moment when they realized the decision framework worked.
**Done when:** A stranger reads it and trusts the founders more than before reading it. It must be SPECIFIC (a real moment, a real person, a real decision) not generic ("we were passionate about helping people").
**Failure modes:**
- Too polished: reads like a PR bio instead of a real story
- No vulnerability: founders only share wins, never the stuck moments
- Doesn't connect to the ICP: her story and their story must rhyme
**Max length:** 1 page
**Confidence:** 🟢 validated (founders have the story, it needs to be captured)

## Quality checklist
- [ ] Emotional thesis makes 4/5 target-demo readers react (agree or argue, not shrug)
- [ ] Every angle has 5+ hook variations tagged by awareness level
- [ ] Voice of Customer section uses zero framework terminology
- [ ] A content writer can produce a week of content using only this document
- [ ] Three voice registers are distinct but all recognizably "Right Decision"
- [ ] Brand vocabulary has customer-facing vs internal usage rules for every term
- [ ] The promise is a transformation, not a feature list
- [ ] Each section ends with "use this when..." instructions
- [ ] Origin story includes a specific moment, not a generic bio
- [ ] The document can generate 50+ unique headlines without repeating
