## From existing documents

### Core thesis (from business model input.md)
"Every meaningful change in your life begins with a decision. Not with information. Not with motivation. Not with a plan. A decision." The decision is the primitive. At any moment, one decision matters more than all others. The singular form IS the thesis.

### Seven angles (from input.md)
1. Decision as atomic unit of change: "You don't have an information problem. You have a decision problem."
2. Decision as diagnosis: "You're making good decisions about the wrong problems."
3. Decision as priority declaration: "If your decision doesn't rearrange your life, it wasn't a decision. It was a wish."
4. Decision as risk acceptance: "If you're waiting until it feels safe, you'll wait forever."
5. Decision as temporal act: "The cost of the wrong decision is visible. The cost of no decision is invisible."
6. Decision as ownership: "No one else can make the right decision for your life."
7. Decision as system: "The right decision isn't a moment. It's a muscle."

### Eight sins (from input.md)
1. Solving the second-biggest constraint because the biggest one is scary
2. Outsourcing the diagnosis
3. Disguising indecision as research
4. Deciding without decomposing
5. Decomposing without deciding
6. Copying someone else's decision
7. Optimizing for reversibility
8. Not deciding, and pretending that's not a decision

### Decision Cycle — 7 steps (from input.md)
State mapping → target state → dominant constraint → decision commitment → decomposition → execution → feedback

### Product name (from input.md)
"The Right Decision" singular. "It forces the audience to confront: you cannot solve everything at once."

### BJ Fogg model (from business model document)
