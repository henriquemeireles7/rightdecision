# RAW FOUNDER ANSWERS — Website & SEO/GEO Strategy

## Q1: Domain topology
**Answer:** Subdirectory model. rightdecisions.io = company site, rightdecisions.io/life = LP, rightdecisions.io/blog = blog, rightdecisions.io/tools = tools. All authority on one domain. Simple routing in Hono.

## Q2: Homepage vision
**Answer:** Manifesto-first. Hero with the core thesis ("The decision is the primitive"), then a brief "what we do" with two cards — Life Decisions (active, links to /life) and Business Decisions (coming soon, grayed). About us teaser, blog highlights, footer with legal links. Think: Basecamp's homepage.

## Q3: Interactive tools strategy
**Answer:** V1: Blog + pages only. Tools in V2 after we have traffic and know what keywords are working. Don't build tools until we have data.

## Q4: Blog content clusters
**Answer:** Mix all three from day one: 1 article per cluster per week. Broader coverage but slower to build topical authority in any one area. Three clusters: feeling stuck, anti-self-help, decision making.

## Q5: Email capture approach
**Answer:** No email capture in V1. Focus purely on SEO and content. Add email capture once you have meaningful traffic. Don't optimize for conversion until you have something to convert.

## Q6: GEO / proprietary concept pages
**Answer:** Glossary + concept pages in V1. Create /concepts/ pages with clear definitions, FAQ schema, and internal links. These become citation targets for AI engines AND pillar pages for SEO clusters. 6-8 pages.

## Q7: V1 scope + concept page naming
**Answer:** Ship the proposed scope: homepage, /life (LP), /about, /privacy, /terms, /blog with first article, /concepts (6-8 pages), sitemap.xml, robots.txt. NOT in V1: tools, email capture, BD pages, free course funnel. 3 articles/week after launch.

**Critical feedback on concept pages:** "We should research keywords and make our concepts more keywords related to what people are searching instead of 'eight sins' — nobody is searching for this. We should have a more SEO-first language into our website." The concept pages should target actual search keywords (e.g., "analysis paralysis", "decision fatigue", "feeling stuck in life") not internal terminology. Proprietary terms get introduced WITHIN the page content, but the URL, H1, and meta title target the search keyword.
