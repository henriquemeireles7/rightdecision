# JTBD Input — Business Decisions Platform

## Raw Founder Thinking (captured 2026-04-07)

### On the ICP

"The 'make money' market is always searching and trying the new thing. I am the first client. And I will be the most active client because I can't keep doing manual stuff anymore. I need to automate EVERYTHING. That is my goal — really have one-person company running and doing billions in revenue without me having to do anything. So my core thesis is the one-person unicorns. That is the JTBD."

### On the two sub-segments

Both operators (have business, want automation) and starters (have idea, need playbook) are the ICP. Same platform, different onboarding. Operators buy for automation, starters buy for the playbook.

### On what they replace

All of these at once:
- Hired VAs/freelancers — expensive, slow, inconsistent quality, still requires management
- No-code tools (Zapier, Make, n8n) — works until it breaks, can't customize, vendor lock-in, not agent-native
- Manual AI chat (ChatGPT/Claude) — powerful but no persistence, no pipeline, rebuilds from scratch every session
- Business courses ($997-4,997) — taught frameworks but gave no working infrastructure, theory without tools

"We are agents-first — we will run our business inside Claude Code — so that means we're using AI + automation. Instead of manual workflows, we're doing it with code. We already do stuff that actually grows the company so no need for pre-AI era business courses. Our course will start $2K but the idea is to move it to $5K in the future."

### On the platform phases

- Phase 1 (Making first money): Content pipeline + Analytics
- Phase 2 (Scaling operation): Other leads (capture + nurturing) → AI Agents (sales + support) → Maybe paid traffic
- Phase 3 (New clients): AI credit system + course creation + online course methodology. "Only in Phase 3 we sell this product."
- Phase 4 (B2B maturity): Payments (Stripe Connect) + multi-teams

### On what comes after the content pipeline

"After we automate content distribution, probably we will understand how to create more content in different ways like AI avatars or code our own OpusClip solution or code our own multi-live solution or a way to use proxies to auto-warm new accounts. The whole idea is to sell more in OUR own funnel. After we have enough clients we will build a Stripe Connect payment stuff so that our users transact into our platform and we also make money with their revenue. The only thing I feel like isn't a direct feature but it would need our attention in the future is having an AI credit system so that we route the APIs inside our platform instead of users needing to use multiple APIs."

### On the emotional job

"Like how I feel right now. I feel like I am playing, not working. And I don't even have everything automated. So the idea is to feel like you 'done it'. Like that feeling that you won in the game of money."

### On the social job

"As someone who lives the life they designed — freedom, autonomy, impact, without the typical founder sacrifice."

### On the anxiety barrier

The #1 fear: "$1,997 is a lot for something I haven't seen work for anyone else yet" — the no-social-proof problem. This is why we build for ourselves first and only sell in Phase 3.

### On what NOT to build

All three:
- A traditional SaaS dashboard — they hired us to ESCAPE clicking buttons in tools
- Done-for-you services — they want to OWN the system, not depend on us
- A generic "start any business" course — they need the SPECIFIC playbook for content-to-revenue with AI
