## From founder

### The trigger: the body as feedback mechanism
The body has a feedback system to get people unstuck. We call them "diseases." Physical or mental, you get sick. The sickness gets worse until you make the right decision. When the disease reaches the point where "the cost of being sick is now bigger than doing the thing," that's the moment they decide. The body IS the trigger.

### The full loop (confirmed)
```
DISCOMFORT/DISEASE
  → Awareness (something is wrong)
  → State Mapping (honest assessment of where you are)
  → Target State (where you want to be, as conditions not metrics)
  → Dominant Constraint (the ONE thing in the way)
  → DECISION (the center of the loop, the primitive)
  → Decomposition (objectives, tasks, habits)
  → Execution (doing the thing, daily)
  → Feedback (what reality tells you)
  → Resolution (constraint solved, specific discomfort gone)
  → NEW DISCOMFORT SURFACES (life is infinite evolution)
```

The body starts the loop. The decision is the center. Resolution is temporary. Life always produces new constraints.

### What resolution feels like
- "The reward is in the execution step. The reward is the execution itself. The reward is to experience the process."
- Indy: "It's a feeling of relief that you are closer to what you want. Like I am already better than yesterday."
- "The level of discomfort is inversely proportional to the ability of making the right decision."
- There is ALWAYS some discomfort. At any time, your level of discomfort is dictated by your decisions.
- "The right decision is also to know at that specific time what you need to do to get the least possible discomfort."
- Solve the constraint → that specific discomfort is gone. But new constraints always come.
- Life is an infinite evolution path.

### When the loop "breaks" (it doesn't)
- The loop NEVER breaks. The disruption is processed through the same cycle.
- "Your old life isn't ready for the new you." Disruptions during progress are normal and expected.
- ANTI-PATTERN: Making the disruption your new dominant constraint. This is deciding from fear and outside events instead of from clarity and inner goals. The constraint is a byproduct of BOTH initial and end state. Ignoring the end state = bad decision.
- ANTI-PATTERN: Pausing the loop to "survive." This means you're not doing the work. You found something else to focus on. People who pause don't come back. They stay stuck for months/years until pain forces restart.
- People fear the unknown. The pain of doing the thing is also the fear of not knowing what will happen.
- CORRECT RESPONSE: Go back to state mapping. Probably don't touch the end state. Understand that when initial or end state changes significantly, the constraint changes too, which means you need a new right decision.
- Rule: If you had a major life change OR you want something different in your end state → redo state mapping, reassess the constraint.
