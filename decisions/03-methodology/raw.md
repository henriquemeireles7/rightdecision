# RAW FOUNDER ANSWERS — Methodology
Captured: 2026-04-04

## Question 1: What triggers the loop? What makes someone go from "fine" to "something needs to change"?

Actualy there is something interesting at play here. The body has a feedback to get people unstuck. We call them 'diseases'. Be it physical or mental, you get sick. Then the sickness gets worse until you make the right decision. So if you don't make the right decision your body will show you that by being sick. Then when the disease is at a point the person starts thinking: "oh the cost of being sick is now bigger than doing the thing", that's the moment they decide to do the thing.

## Question 2: Full loop confirmation

The loop starts with discomfort/disease and ends when the constraint is resolved and a new discomfort surfaces. Confirmed:

Discomfort/disease → awareness → state mapping → target state → dominant constraint → DECISION → decomposition → execution → feedback → resolution → new discomfort surfaces

The body is the starting mechanism and the decision is the center.

## Question 3: What does resolution feel like? How long before the next cycle?

Its like the dopamine cycle. The reward is in the execution step, the reward is the execution itself, the reward is to experience the proccess. My wife: its a feeling of relief that you are closer to what you want, like I am already better than yesterday. The level of discomfort is inversely proportional to the ability of making the right decision. So there is always some discomfort, but at any specific time your level of discomfort will be dictated by your decisions. So the right decision is also to know at that specific time what you need to do to get the least possible discomfort - that means that the constraint always changes and produces a different type of discomfort. Solve the constraint, that specific discomfort is gone. But life is an infinite evolution path, so there will always be new constraints.

## Question 4: When the loop breaks / life derails the process

This is like if your 'old life' isn't ready for the 'new you', so there are situations that will happen to make you go back into your previous constraint. Its normal and expected when you are having progress. The "loop doesn't break" option is the best way to explain exactly the problem. People make the disruption their new dominant constraint and thats is actualy a big problem, because then they will make a new decision based only on this new constraint, but remember that the constraint is a byproduct of both the initial and the end state, so when you ignore the end state you are making a bad decision. You are deciding from fear and from outside events instead of deciding from clarity and inner goals. Also the second answer is anti-pattern, because the whole idea of the loop is that you are always in the loop, so if you just pause it to survive what it means is that you are not doing the work. You again found something to focus on instead of doing the work. And people do that, they pause it and focus inwards but they don't come back, then they again stay stuck for months/years and only restart the cycle when everything gets worse at that inflexion point where the pain of not doing the thing is smaller than the actual life. Also another factor interesting is that people fear the unknown, so the pain of doing the thing is also related to the fear of not knowing what will happen when you do the thing. So the whole idea is that when someone gets stuck or life throws something that derails them they should go back into state mapping, probably don't touch their end state, but understand that the more you change your initial or end state, there will be a bigger disruption into the actual constraint, that makes so you need a new right decision because the constraint changed. Maybe when getting fired the constraint isn't not having enough time anymore, the constraint evolved as the situation evolved. So people should go back into state mapping during execution if their state (initial or end) changes in a way that the constraint doesn't make sense anymore. Its hard to put a number to it because this is subjective, but is you had a major life change or you want something different in your end state, you should redo the state mapping and reassess the constraint.
