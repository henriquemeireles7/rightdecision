# Social Post Rules

## Platforms
- LinkedIn (primary — ICP is there)
- Instagram (secondary — visual + reels)
- X/Twitter (tertiary — thought leadership)

## Structure (per platform)

### LinkedIn (max 3,000 chars)
1. **Hook line** — First line must stop the scroll (visible before "see more")
2. **Story or observation** — 3-5 short paragraphs
3. **Insight** — What this teaches about decisions
4. **CTA** — Question or invitation (never "check out my product")

### Instagram (caption max 2,200 chars)
1. **Hook** — First line (visible before truncation)
2. **Value** — 2-3 paragraphs
3. **CTA** — "Link in bio" or question
4. **Hashtags** — 5-10 relevant, mix of sizes

### X/Twitter (max 280 chars per tweet)
1. Single tweet: one insight, one question, or one provocative statement
2. Thread: 3-7 tweets, each standalone but building
3. No hashtags in tweets (looks spammy)

## Content Types
- **Observation** — "I noticed that..." (personal, specific)
- **Counter-intuitive** — "Everyone says X. But..." (anti-self-help)
- **Story** — Specific moment that illustrates a principle
- **Question** — One question that makes people stop and think
- **Framework** — A simple model explained in 3 steps

## Writing Style
- Conversational, not polished
- Short sentences. Line breaks between thoughts.
- No emojis at sentence starts (LinkedIn spam signal)
- Specific > general ("I coached 400 founders" not "I've helped many people")
- Anti-self-help tone: no "journey", "growth mindset", "manifesting"

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Hook line works standalone (scroll-stopping)
- [ ] No AI-sounding language
- [ ] Platform-appropriate length
- [ ] CTA is a question or invitation, not a pitch
- [ ] Would pass the "would Henry actually post this?" test
