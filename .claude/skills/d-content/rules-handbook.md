# Handbook Rules

## Purpose
Handbook entries are practical reference guides for the Right Decision methodology.
They teach ONE concept deeply, with exercises the reader can do immediately.

## Structure
1. **What this is** (1-2 sentences) — Define the concept in plain language
2. **Why it matters** — Connect to the ICP's real situation (stuck, overwhelmed, "done the work")
3. **How it works** — The methodology step, explained simply
4. **Exercise** — A specific, timed exercise (e.g., "Take 10 minutes. Write down...")
5. **Example** — A real or realistic example showing the exercise completed
6. **Common mistakes** — 2-3 pitfalls and how to avoid them
7. **What's next** — Link to the next handbook entry or course module

## Writing Style
- Instructional but warm — like a mentor, not a textbook
- Second person ("you") throughout
- Present tense for instructions
- Specific time estimates for exercises ("This takes 10 minutes")
- Anti-self-help tone: action over introspection

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Teaches ONE concept (not three crammed together)
- [ ] Exercise is specific and timed
- [ ] Example is realistic (not a perfect-world scenario)
- [ ] Common mistakes are from real user patterns
- [ ] Voice.md compliant (story-first, specific, rhythm variation)
- [ ] Under 2,000 words (handbook = concise reference)
